Department of Surgical Oncology

The department of Surgical Oncology comprises of the sub specialities of head and Neck Surgery, Breast and Thoracic surgery, and GI Surgery. In addition there are plans to add several more specialities like Gynaec Oncology, Uro- oncology etc. This makes it unique in this part of the country.

The department works with the concept of Tumor Board, where cases are put up for a multidisciplinary review including specialists from Medical Oncology, Radiation Oncology, Pathology, Radiology and any other allied branch that can help in decision-making process for that patient. This is another unique advantage that BMC patients have in the region, and ensures that the treatment is in accordance with international guidelines.

Department of Nuclear Medicine

Nuclear medicine is a medical specialty involving the application of radioactive substances in the diagnosis and treatment of disease. At BMC, we have both SPECT CT & PET CT for imaging and also offer treatment as well using Radioiodine HDT & LDT therapy.

Department of Medical Oncology

Medical Oncology is the use of specialized medications, referred to as chemotherapy drugs, to control and eradicate cancerous cells throughout a patient’s body. A medical oncologist is the physician responsible for the management and oversight of a patients Chemotherapy regimen, and the complications which may be arise over the course of several weeks, months, or years.

The Department deals with chemotherapy planning, preparation and administration as per the standard guidelines (NCCN guidelines, NICE guidelines, Cancer therapy advisor protocols) in our 20 bedded day care centre.

At BALCO Medical Centre, all types of chemotherapy administration are conducted including:

  • Conventional chemotherapy
  • Concurrent chemotherapy with radiation
  • Targeted therapy
  • Biological therapy
  • Intrathecal chemotherapy
  • Intraperitoneal and intrathoracic cavity chemo
  • OPD services and second opinion clinic

Pain & Palliative Care

Palliative care helps people with physical (pain and other distressing symptoms), psychological, social, spiritual and practical issues as well as coping with loss and grief during the illness and bereavement.

​In the most difficult last moments of life, palliative care ensures that patients receive care that reflects their values and wishes so they can live with the highest quality of life possible and ultimately, die comfortably where they choose. In contrast to the common misconception regarding palliative care being offered only in the last days of life, present day practice involves palliative care being offered alongside care intended to prolong survival which has ensured significantly better quality of life for the patient and their family and improved survival.

​Even for patients with a terminal cancer for whom death appears to be imminent, focusing on good symptom control may lead to comfortable and dignified life till the time they breathe their last. Acceptance of death is the starting point for a conversation with patient regarding involvement of palliative care for good end of life care.

Services:

  • Out Patient Clinic (Daily)
  • Round the clock Acute Pain Services

Radiation Oncology

Radiation Oncology involves the controlled use of radiation to treat cancer. Recent advancements in radiation therapy have revolutionized the treatment of cancer, leading to better clinical outcomes for patients. A specialty physician called a Radiation Oncologist manages and oversees each patient’s radiation treatment. Only a radiation oncologist can help in determining if radiation therapy is right for the patient.

There are two ways by which radiation can be administered to patients:

  • External Beam Radiation:External beam radiation therapy delivers radiation through the use of a large machine called a linear accelerator that precisely aims radiation beams from outside of the body at the cancerous tumor.
  • Brachytherapy or Intra-Cavitary Radiation Therapy:Brachytherapy is a type of radiation therapy in which radioactive particles known as seeds are placed within the body on the tumor or nearby the cancer cells.

At BMC, we have a state of the art complete Radiation setup with:

  • TrueBeam Linear Accelerator, the first of its kind in the Madhya Pradesh – Chhattisgarh area
  • GammaMedPlus iX: HDR Brachytherapy with 24 channels

About half of all cancer patients receive some type of radiation therapy at some point during the course of their cancer care.
